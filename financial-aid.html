Types of aid

Ways to fund
your program.

Grants you do not repay, federal loans, veterans benefits, and FEI own options, our Financial Aid Office will walk you through which ones apply to you.

A federal grant, money you do not pay back, for students who qualify based on financial need, determined through the FAFSA.

An additional federal grant for students with exceptional financial need. Like the Pell Grant, it does not need to be repaid.

Low-interest federal student loans. Subsidized loans are need-based, with interest covered while you are in school; unsubsidized loans are available regardless of need. Learn more at studentaid.gov.

Federal loans that parents of dependent undergraduate students can use to help cover education costs.

FEI welcomes veterans and eligible dependents. Post-9/11 GI Bill students need a VA Certificate of Eligibility (COE); Vocational Rehabilitation students need form VAF 28-1905. Details at benefits.va.gov/gibill.

FEI own institutional loan option, ask the Financial Aid Office whether it fits your situation.

Scholarships

Two FEI
scholarships.

Awarded by the FEI Scholarship Committee. Ask the Financial Aid Office for full details and the school catalog.

High School Student Scholarship, $2,500 Four awards per year (two in winter, two in fall), valid for all FEI career programs. For graduating high-school seniors with a cumulative GPA of 2.5+.

To apply: online application, a 500-word essay on your career goals and why FEI, one school/professional reference letter, and one family reference letter.
Deadlines: January 15 and May 15.
The Luis Alvarez CPA Scholarship Award A memorial scholarship honoring FEI founding accountant. The number of awards varies each year with available funding, details in the school catalog and at the Financial Aid Office.
