What does a Medical Office Administrator do?

The business side
of patient care.

Medical Office Administrators keep healthcare running behind the scenes, managing records, billing, insurance claims, and the daily operations that let clinicians focus on patients. If you are organized, like problem-solving, and want a healthcare career built around business and administration rather than clinical work, this associate degree is designed for you.

Curriculum

What you will master

  • Medical Office Procedures
  • Medical Terminology
  • Anatomy and Physiology
  • Medical Billing and Coding
  • Electronic Health Records
  • Insurance Claims and Reimbursement
  • Healthcare Office Administration
  • Patient Communication and Customer Service
  • Medical Law, Ethics, and Office Compliance
